Who am I?

Hi! I'm Michelle Zhuang. I'm a junior at Swarthmore College majoring in Engineering and Computer Science.

At Swarthmore, I have previously served as Student Academic Mentor, advising almost 100 students to ensure their academic success.

Currently, I am a Resident Assistant, managing administrative duties, such as budgeting, and fostering a community among a cohort of ethnically diverse undergraduate students.

I am also an active member in the Women in Computer Science club (WiCS) on campus, building a community to empower other women with a passion for technology.

A Passion to Create

Alongside my passion for technology, I have a passion to create. These passions of mine have motivated my project for the Summer of 2021, where I hope to find an intersection of both - more information on that can be found in the navigation menu above!

Another passion of mine is baking! While baking may seem irrelevant to the topics of technology and innovation, I believe it is a great way to express my strengths and show my capability for hands-on innovation (plus it is a deligiously rewarding treat). Baking depicts my determination and diligence as I recreate a recipe til I get it right - afterall, you know what they say: "Baking is a science." I also believe it depicts my creativity as I come up with recipes from what combinations of flavors and ingredients spark interest in me.
