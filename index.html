Who am I?

I’m Michelle Zhuang, a software engineer with hands-on experience in building data pipelines, automating workflows, and solving complex problems at companies like Cisco and Oracle. I’m passionate about using my technical skills in Python, Kafka, and PL/SQL to develop innovative solutions that streamline processes and make a tangible impact.

Beyond my technical work, I’m always eager to learn and challenge myself, whether it’s through leading technical projects, exploring new technologies like machine learning and computer vision, or designing unique solutions, like a pattern-making program that integrates with Autodesk Fusion 360.

I am a graduate from Swarthmore College, double majoring in Engineering and Computer Science. At Swarthmore, I have previously served as the President of Women in Computer Science club (WiCS) on campus, building a community to empower other women with a passion for technology. My passion for community building doesn't stop there. I was also a Resident Assistant, managing administrative duties, such as budgeting, and fostering a community among a cohort of ethnically diverse undergraduate students.
